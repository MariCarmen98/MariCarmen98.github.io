YUNA, A MICROPLASTIC FILTERING BUOY

Yuna is the redesign of a common buoy which includes a microplastic filtering system, with the objective of using it to clean our seas and oceans from microplastics. It is a group project conformed by 20 students that grew in 2018 in an area of the university that encourages its students to create new and innovative projects, by themselves. It is still an active project, that continues to be developed by 4 of us.

Yuna requires little energy to fulfill its function. It has an aerodynamic structure inspired by the anthropometry of the moonfish, due to the easy adaptation of its shape to the flow of the tide. The microplastics that travel through the currents will reach the in- terior of the buoy that will rotate according to the currents, like a weather vane. The fil- tering system rests on a series of filters from highest to lowest grammage that will filter the microplastics according to their size. Filtration by activated carbon and other agents is proposed for smaller particles, mi- crons in size. Once the microplastics are fil- tered, they will be reused so that they do not return to the sea. The product will be made of polyethylene whose degradation does not imply contamination of the environment, achieving a sustainable and circular product. It is proposed to reduce costs by making the two pieces of the external structure of the buoy with a single mold.

It is an idea with spreading intentions. It already was:
Represented in official presentations (TEDx talk and interviews)
Recognised as winner project in some competitions: National winner in James Dyson Award 2019, winner in VI Challenge Ideas UPV (in the Ecology & Environment category) and Start UPV 2k19 (in the Business Project category).

1.Research: During the first two months, we developed some research so to define the problem, the scope and the kind of product we wanted to come up with.

2.Conceptualisation phase: Once the scope was defined, we worked on different brainstorming sessions and creative methods. We defined criteria from the previous reserarch inpunts and choose the most promising idea from which we wanted to iterate from then on.
3.Embodiment phase: Once the concept was defined, we worked on its embodiment, by defining its shape, materials, realising a smaller scale model, testing different opening systems, etc.

4.Commercialization proposal: Simultaneously with this last phase, we work in spreading the idea by increasing the awareness on microplastics and on its possible comercialization.
